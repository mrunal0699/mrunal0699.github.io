Can We Just Be Human First?

The other day, I had a conversation with someone that really shook me. He casually said something like, "Women being at home with no freedom was better."

At first, I was deeply offended. As a woman, hearing that felt like someone punched me in the chest. How could anyone think like that in this era? But later, when I sat with my thoughts and really tried to understand where such statements come from—not agree with them, but understand—a part of me saw the painful truth behind it. Not that women belong in cages or that they deserve no freedom. No. But that we’ve reached a point where freedom has started looking like chaos, and that’s something we need to talk about.

Now before anyone gets mad or offended—please hear me out. I’m not saying I fully agree with that statement. I’m just saying... maybe we should reflect on why things feel so broken nowadays. I’m just sharing my thoughts, and if I hurt anyone in the process, I truly apologize. I'm open to discussions, and I would love to learn from your perspective too.

The Labels We Live By

Let’s be real for a second—how many times have we seen quotes like “Men will be men” or “All women are gold diggers”? These things are everywhere. Memes, social media, jokes, everyday conversations. Slowly, without even realizing, we’ve started believing them.

Men are labeled as cheaters. Women are labeled as manipulators. Men are scared of getting married because they think, “What if she only cares about money or doesn’t support my family?” Women are scared of marriage because they think, “What if he cheats on me, tries to control me, or emotionally abuses me?”

And the truth is, both fears are valid—because we’ve all seen stories, heard examples, or even experienced them firsthand. But here’s the thing: not all men are the same. Not all women are the same.

And it’s unfair that we’ve boxed people into these stereotypes just because of someone else’s mistakes.

It’s Not a Gender Thing—It’s a Human Thing

Some women misuse their rights. Some men abuse their power. Some women manipulate. Some men cheat. But this isn’t a gender problem—it’s a people problem. A human problem.

We’re so busy proving who’s right—men or women—that we’ve forgotten that both can be wrong. And more importantly, both can be good.

Why can’t we just look at each other like humans first? Not as a man or a woman, but as a person who wants love, peace, freedom, and respect.

What Equality Really Means

Equality is not just about giving women the right to work or letting men cry. It’s about creating a space where a woman can be strong without being called "too much" and a man can be vulnerable without being called "weak."

It’s about understanding the other person. It’s about listening before judging. It’s about stopping the blame game and just being human with one another.

A Small Hope

I don’t have a conclusion to this blog. Because life isn’t a conclusion—it’s a process. And I’m still learning.

But I do have a hope.

A hope that someday, we’ll raise children who don’t grow up learning phrases like “men are trash” or “women are gold diggers.” A hope that relationships will be built on trust and not fear. A hope that we’ll start conversations that matter—even the uncomfortable ones—because only then can we heal.

So if you’ve read this far, thank you. And if you have a different opinion, I would genuinely love to hear it. Not to argue, but to understand.

Because that’s what being human is all about.
